1.
Overview of the Case Study

This case study focuses on analyzing the sales performance of Bright Coffee Shop using transactional sales data.
The dataset is for business operations across multiple store locations and includes detailed information on sales transactions, product categories, revenue, time of purchase, and customer spending behavior.

The purpose of the case study was to use data analysis techniques to evaluate how the business performs, identify major revenue drivers, understand customer behavior, and uncover trends related to time and location.
The findings of this case study are intended to Help the CEO to make  business decision‑making, improve operational efficiency, and identify opportunities for revenue growth.

2. OBJECTIVE

Use your analytics, SQL, and data visualization skills to help Bright Coffee Shop understand:

• Which products generate the most revenue

• What time of day the store performs best

• Sales trends across products and time intervals

• Recommendations for improving sales performance

4. How the Case Study Was Conducted and Insights Found
4.1 How the Analysis Was Conducted
The case study was conducted using a structured analytical approach:

Data Inspection 
The Excel dataset was reviewed to understand its structure, variables, and scope. The data included fields such as transaction date and time, product category, product type, store location, quantity sold.
 
Then , the data was uploaded on databricks - Data Summarization and Aggregation to Calculate total sales and total revenue and also to classify by time , month , day and time of the day.
Databricks was used to:

Analyze revenue across different time intervals

Evaluate performance by store location

Identify daily and monthly revenue patterns

Trend and Pattern Analysis : Sales patterns were examined by: Time of day (Morning Peak, Lunch Peak, Afternoon, Evening) ,Day of the week ,Month and Customer spend classification



5.1 Insight Interpretation
The numerical findings were translated into meaningful business insights. These insights were later summarized into a presentation.



Key Insights Identified

The following insights were identified from the analysis:

Coffee is the primary revenue driver, contributing the highest sales volume and revenue.

Tea is the second strongest category, showing consistent demand.

Bakery items act as strong add‑on products, increasing basket size.

Morning Peak (07:00–11:00) generates the highest sales and revenue.

Lunch Peak (11:30–13:30) is the second most profitable period.

Hell’s Kitchen is the top‑performing store overall.

Astoria performs particularly well in bakery and drinking chocolate sales.

Lower Manhattan shows balanced performance across categories.

Most customers are classified as low spenders, typically purchasing one or two items per transaction.
